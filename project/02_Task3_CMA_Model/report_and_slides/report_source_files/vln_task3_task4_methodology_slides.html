Task 4 Methodology

Generalization and Ablation Study

We tested whether the learned CMA policy still works when the environment, instruction wording, training data size, or fusion mechanism changes.
Evaluate → Stress Test → Compare → Interpret
1

Held-Out Layouts

Evaluated outside the training route subset to check whether the policy transfers beyond familiar navigation patterns.

2

Paraphrases

Generated rule-based instruction rewrites such as walk → move and hallway → corridor to test language robustness.

3

Reduced Data

Compared smaller and larger trajectory subsets to measure how route diversity affects rollout behavior.

4

Fusion Ablation

Compared CMA cross-attention against simpler gated visual-language fusion under a matched small-data setting.

What the results mean

  • CMA reduced final distance much more effectively than gated fusion, suggesting token-level grounding is important.
  • More training trajectories improved validation rollout performance, showing that route diversity matters.
  • Paraphrasing exposed language sensitivity: the model understands useful patterns but remains wording-dependent.
  • Held-out layouts were harder, mainly because of over-turning and weak stopping calibration in unfamiliar scenes.
